The Unobtainium Project

The keys are gone.
The legacy isn’t.

A growing archive of solid, true-to-life CAD models of legendary CW keys that no longer exist — preserved and given freely, so the keys that came before us never truly fall silent.

Free to download — solid models, no strings

Why we built this

Every operator who keys up today is standing on the shoulders of giants. The benchmakers and basement machinists, the engineers who chased a perfect contact across a lifetime, the elmers who handed down a paddle along with the patience to use it — they built the feel we all reach for. Many of them are silent keys now. Their workbenches went quiet; their tooling was scattered; and one by one, the keys they made slipped out of the world.

A great key is not just brass and steel. It is the distilled judgment of the man who made it — the radius he settled on after a thousand contacts, the heft he chose because it felt right under a working fist. When the last of a model crumbles, restoration becomes guesswork, and a piece of the craft is lost for good. We refuse to let that happen quietly.

We cannot bring the makers back. But we can keep their work measured, honored, and within reach — so no operator ever has to let a legend rot for want of a single missing paddle.

So we measure them. We reverse-engineer the survivors — calipers, scans, and stubborn patience — and rebuild each one as a clean, solid CAD model, dimensioned the way its maker would have drawn it. Then we give it away. No paywall, no gatekeeping. This is our way of repaying a debt to the men who came before us, and of putting something back into the hobby that gave us so much.

How to use the models

1

Download the solid model

Each key is shared as a watertight solid — true geometry, not a hollow mesh — ready to open in the CAD tool you already use.

2

Build, fit, or restore

Reference the exact dimensions to machine a replacement paddle, design a custom base or case, or 3D-print a part that’s no longer sold anywhere on earth.

3

Put a legend back on the air

Bring a tired key back to life, or carry its lines forward into something new. Either way, the maker’s work keys up again.

The pact we keep

Honor the silent keys

Every model is a small act of remembrance — the work of an SK, measured with respect and carried forward intact.

Free, and freely given

No paywall stands between an operator and a part he needs. We give these models back to the community, the way the craft was given to us.

Faithful to the maker

We model what was, not what we’d change. The geometry stays true to the original so restorations stay honest.

On the shoulders of giants

We build new keys because better men taught us how. Preserving theirs is how we say thank you — and how we earn our place in the line.
